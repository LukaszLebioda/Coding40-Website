Adaptability

Comes with: Resourcefulness, Troubbleshooting skills

You'd rather sit around at home with a hot cup of tea in one hand and a pipe in the other but they tell You to leave all of this behind and set for an adventure to the edges of the world... Adapt quickly or perish like snows of yesteryear.

Worlf of IT changes rapidly, technologies come and go, inventions replace inventions, and You just can't stick with one solution to a problem because soon enough there will be another solution to another problem.

Emotional intelligence

Comes with: Empathy

Overworked and underpaid level of intelligence that is actually quite useful both in job and relationship contexts. Might come in handy when negotiating a new contract with supervisors, an execution of some deadline tasks with colleagues, as well as a postponing of housekeeping duties with partners.

Being emotionally intelligent means being capable of mind-reading. Use it wise, e.g. to reach a common company goal, never to Your own advantage.

Task-orientated

Comes with: Ability to concentrate

You shouldn't let Yourself drift away to the meanders of Your mind when in the middle of a mind-breaking, time-sensitive project. Just keep the ultimate goal within an arms' reach and never let go. Don't get Yourself distracted by little things.

On the other hand if You feel You're losing it, give it a break for some time. You're just a human after all, not some creature from a fantasy world.

Analytical

Comes with: Investigative, inquiring

It's good to have a broader knowledge about the surrounding universe. It's even better to know where to find this knowledge. But the best option would be to know how to use and deepen this knowledge when a problem occurs.

To be able to profoundly analyze a problem and find a corresponding solution is a precious gift. Don't spend too much time analizing though, for there are more problems waiting.
