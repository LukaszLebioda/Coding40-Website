Fuel counter

Scenario: You and Your friends go on a voyage with Your car. Hardly were You able to agree one specific weekend when You all have some free time and now You can go! You don't want to end up like a bunch of math students however, waving around with sheets of paper and pencils on a parking lot in the middle of nowhere, arguing how much each of You should pay for fuel.
How is this useful: Basing upon provided data the program will count how much each member of the expedition should share for fuel.
Disclaimer: Prices of petrol change rapidly. You might want to use this program before the trip, as well as after, just to compare...
Source code: This is a second program I wrote in Java Script. Nothing fancy actually, but quite useful I think. I feel there are to many restrictions as far as user's input data is concerned, however this is something I'll improve in the upcoming programs hopefully. Download this app from my GitHub repository or just hit npm i fuelcounter in Your terminal.

Bird recognizer

Scenario: Normally You just don't pay attention. There are so many things to do, to remember, to deal with... On Your way to work or home You just think intensively about all of that without even realizing how diverse and beautiful the world outside the car actually is. But there comes a moment when You spot a colorful bird walking on the top of the traffic light and You start wondering what the heck is that.
How is this useful: There are 467 speciecies of birds either living in Poland all year long, coming in spring to reproduce and raise babies or just passing through, ocasionally landing to search for food. In your surroundings You may spot 10-20 most common species daily. Unless You're professional ornithologist You may find it hard figure out what are You actually looking at. This program will help You!
Disclaimer: Birds are creatures of nature. They may behave randomly, unexpectedly, beyond our perception and comprehension. This program (altough the most complicated I've written so far) will not encompass every species, every situation, every circumstance. Do not make bets with Your friends relying solely on the results provided. You may lose and I'm not keen on refunding.
Source code: I used arrays, objects, for loops, addEventListeners, querySelectors and many more for the first time in my life. My God, this was equally depressive and satisfactory trying to put all of these it into motion! Download this app from my GitHub repository or just hit npm i birdrecognizer in Your terminal.
