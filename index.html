Lifecycle

There are 6 stages of sotware development

Well... It depends, but mostly there are.

#1 Analysis

I fell into this vast IT-ocean, I'm in the middle of nowhere, I don't know what to do, and dont't know where to go. I need a raft and a paddle. I need a website to forge an order out of all that chaos my mind was turned into.

#2 Design

What is required? Strength to change what can be changed, humility to accept unchangeable and enough reason to tell the difference. And some tools'n'software. Some hardskills, some softskills as well. But most of all: time. A hell of a lot of time.

#3 Development

A beginner's code is like a little brother - bit annoying, bit messy, sometimes unpredictive, sometimes repetitive. But in the end of the day You keep playing with it and see it grows up. It might become somebody. Sky is the limit! Or at least they say it is...

#4 Testing

Specified requirements to verify when all of this is over: understanding modern world, channelling creativity, having fun, inspiring others who hesitate or have little faith. Needs and expectations to validate: having better career oportunities.

#5 Deployment

You write stories for somebody to read them, possibly even enjoy them. But You're so afraid Your work comes to light. So You stash all those handwritten sheets of paper at the bottom of Your desk drawer. Enough of that. It's hightime You finally did it.

#6 Maintenance

A superior power is said to have created this world and left it alone for the entropy to do its job. It's probably easier this way. But it's not something I'm gonna do. I will water this plant untill it blossoms. I mean I think I will.
